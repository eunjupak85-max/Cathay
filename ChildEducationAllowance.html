Child Education Allowance (Hong Kong)

Cathay Staff Notice • Monthly Grant + Additional Portion (Top-Up) Tracking

1) Policy Objective

To support HK-based employees with dependent children’s education costs. The Child Education Allowance (CEA) provides a monthly grant based on the number of nominated eligible children. When actual tuition exceeds monthly caps, an Additional Portion (Top-Up) may be requested and tracked separately.

2) Eligibility

Eligible Employees

  • Permanent or fixed-term employees based in Hong Kong (per internal grade/role criteria).
  • Under an approved legal entity/organization and pay group.

Eligible Children

  • Legally dependent children below 19, or up to 23 if in full-time study.
  • Enrolled in a recognized institution in Hong Kong or an approved location.
  • Proof of dependency required.

Exact thresholds are defined in HR guidance. Exceptions require HR approval.

3) Monthly Grant (Automatic)

The monthly CEA grant is automatically paid via payroll once the number of eligible children is nominated and approved in SuccessFactors. No monthly claim submission is required.

4) Nomination (in SuccessFactors)

  • Submit a Child Nomination in SuccessFactors (Global Benefits) indicating the number of eligible children and school level.
  • Required documents:
    • Proof of dependency (e.g., birth certificate) – on first registration.
    • Proof of enrollment / tuition receipt – each academic year/term.
  • Report any change (transfer, deferment, graduation) within 30 days.

5) Additional Portion (Top-Up) — Separate Tracking & Payment

If actual tuition exceeds the monthly cap, employees may request an Additional Portion (Top-Up). This is not part of the automatic grant and is paid as a separate, non-recurring item after approval.

  • When used: International schools or special fees above the monthly cap.
  • Approval: Department Head and HR pre-approval required (via SF workflow).
  • Payment type: Non-recurring pay component (e.g., CEA_TOPUP), separate from the monthly grant (CEA_MONTHLY).
  • Documentation: Detailed tuition invoice/receipt; may require term schedule and breakdown.
  • Audit: Subject to year-end review; overpayments recovered per HKEO limits.
Example: Secondary cap HKD 6,000; actual tuition HKD 8,500 ⇒ monthly grant HKD 6,000 (automatic), Top-Up HKD 2,500 (approved separately).

6) Controls & Recovery

  • Carry-forward: Not applicable (non-accruing monthly grant).
  • Overpayment recovery: In line with HKEO deduction limits (up to 25% of wages per period; total deductions capped at 50%).

7) Cessation

  • Child ceases full-time study or is no longer a legal dependent.
  • Employment ends or transfer outside HK eligibility.
  • HR review determines ineligibility.
